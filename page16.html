While carrying out usability testing you could be inclined to play the role of a facilitator. There to roles played by a facilitator

  • A tour guide: responsible for telling the participants what to do and keeping them moving. But your role is not to give your participants all the answers but rather to let them figure out the solution to the problem.
  • A therapist: responsible for getting your participant to verbal express themselves. You are going to get your participants to think out loud as much as possible

Organizing the test room

  • Have a computer with internet access and screen recording software
  • A monitor and keyboard, will be important if you are using a laptop. This will allow you to see the screen of your participants without having to be too close.
  • A good mouse to allow the participant to easily navigate the webpage. The laptop touch pad might be a big inconvenience to the participant.
  • Have a USB microphone, this will allow you to get good quality audio from the observation room. It also prevents you from straining your ear trying to listener to what someone has to easy.
  • A speakerphone, will allow you to communicate well with all the participants in your testing room.
  • Screen sharing, will allow you to easily direct your participants before or during the testing stages.
  • Turnoff any software that could distract the user or participants.

How to conduct usability testing

  • Try to get the user to externalist his or her thoughts
  • Try not to influence their decision
  • Stay neutral give them no instructions during the testing phase to maximize how much you can learn about you user.
