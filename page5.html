Omit unnecessary words

This is simply because too many words make the web page seem intimidating and might cause users not to be interested in reading them.

You can do this by removing half the words, which creates the effect listed below

  • Reduces the noise level of the page
  • Makes the useful content more readable and easier to find
  • Makes the page shorter allow users to see more of the page without having to scroll.

Happy talk must die

This is the content on a web page that is designed to introduce its readers to the website, by telling them how good it is and what they expect to see in most of the sections.

The problem with happy talk it that it doesn’t have any useful information and usually focuses on say how good things are.

Most web users don’t have any time for happy talk all they want to do is get what brought them to the website. It’ important not to include happy talk in your webpage if you want to keep the website as simple as possible.

Instructions must die.

Simply because most web users don’t read instructions it’s important not to include instructions onto your webpage.

You can eliminate instructions on your webpage entirely while making most of the webpage self-explanatory. So, while users are muddling, they are more likely to get a correct response from your website.
