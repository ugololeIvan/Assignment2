How we really use the web

There is a big difference between, how people view websites and how we think people view websites. It’s common for you to believe, that users viewing your website are going to look at very part of the web page. And when you are creating the webpage you might make some parts of your webpage have a lot of content.

But what most people are used to doing is just glace through the website while going through the first most interesting thing they can find.

The reason why you might mistakenly create a website with a lot of content “most of the content condensed in one area” is that you, or anybody else for that matter might assume that we are designing webpages for people who use websites the way we do – or that the large majority of our users are rational and attentive. But that is not usually the case.When trying to design any web page you have to put into consideration the three facts of using a webpage, and these are;

Most people don’t read web pages they scan through them.

Most people don’t spend most of their time reading though all the content on the web page they just scan through it. Looking for key words and phrases that might catch their eye. This only applies if the webpage is not a news website or sports website, for such websites users usually read them but might revert to scanning periodically.
